# Law of Proximity

"Objects that are near, or proximate to each other, tend to be grouped together."

## Takeaways

- Proximity helps establish a relationship between nearby objects.
- Elements in close proximity are perceived to share similar functionality or traits.
- Proximity helps users understand and organize information faster and more efficiently.

## Overview

The Law of Proximity is one of the Gestalt principles of grouping. The human visual system reads spatial distance as meaning: items pushed together are read as belonging to one unit, items pushed apart are read as belonging to different units. This is why whitespace is a structural tool, not a decorative one.

A canonical example is a search results page. Title, URL, and snippet sit tightly together while generous space separates each result. No lines or boxes are needed for users to know where one result ends and the next begins.

## Origins

The grouping principles come from Gestalt psychology in the early 20th century. Gestalt psychologists argued that the mind has an innate tendency to perceive stimuli as organized patterns ([Prägnanz](law-of-pragnanz.md)) governed by specific rules. The five classical grouping categories are Proximity, Similarity, Continuity, Closure, and Connectedness.
